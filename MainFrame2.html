Welcome to Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery!

A turtle hatchery is a facility that provides a safe environment for sea turtle eggs to hatch, protecting them from natural and human threats. Once the hatchlings are born, they are kept in the hatchery until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean, contributing to the conservation of endangered sea turtles.

What do these little guys eat?

Turtles are omnivores and their diet varies depending on their species, age, and environment. Most turtles eat a combination of animal and plant-based foods, such as:

  • insects
  • fish
  • snails
  • fruits
  • vegetables

Some turtles are strictly herbivores and eat only plant-based foods, while others are carnivores and feed on other small animals. A well-balanced diet is important for the health and longevity of turtles.

Where are they found?

Sri Lanka is home to several species of turtles, including:

  1. Olive Ridley
  2. Green turtle
  3. Loggerhead turtle
  4. Hawksbill turtle
  5. Leatherback turtle

These turtles can be found nesting on the beaches along the southern and eastern coasts of Sri Lanka, particularly in the towns of:

  1. Bentota
  2. kosgoda
  3. Rekawa

Many of these nesting sites have been designated as protected areas by the Sri Lankan government, and visitors can witness the nesting and hatching of turtles through guided tours and conservation programs.
